Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about water testing, filtration systems, and maintenance.

What is included in your free analysis?
Our free water analysis tests for hardness, iron, pH, chlorine, TDS (total dissolved solids), manganese, and sulfur (hydrogen sulfide). If you have an existing filter system, we will also check that it is working properly.
What else can you test for?
We offer testing services for just about any contaminant, including pesticides, MTBE, lead, mercury, other heavy metals, and chemicals. We can come to your home or business to take the sample for you.
Are your systems guaranteed?
Every system includes the manufacturer’s warranty. Warranty periods vary by unit and manufacturer (typically one to ten years).
How often should my system be serviced?
Service schedules vary by system, but most systems should be checked at least once per year. UV lights and reverse osmosis systems especially benefit from annual service.
What is a backwash/regeneration?
Many filter tanks and softeners periodically clean themselves (every 2 days to several weeks, depending on the system). This is called backwashing or regeneration. It typically runs when water isn’t being used (often around midnight or 2am). During the cycle, accumulated sediment and “filter load” are flushed out through a drain line leading to a drywell, septic, or sewage system. Afterward, the unit is restored to a cleaner state and ready for service again.
How do I set my clock?
Units that backwash usually have a clock that determines when the cycle runs. After a power loss, reset the clock to the current time of day. Instructions are typically on the clock face or in your owner’s manual. Set it for the time of day — not the time the tank should backwash.
How do I shock my well?
For proper well shocking procedures, contact your local Board of Health Department or follow published guidance from a trusted extension program.
